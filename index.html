"A guide to Ilocos Sur Philippines"

Ilocos Sur

Welcome to Ilocos Sur, one of the foremost historically wealthy areas within the nation that blends the colorful Ilocano culture with colonial Spanish legacy. Known for its centuries-old design, Ilocos Sur is the culminate goal for history buffs and inquisitive travelers alike.
Not one, but two UNESCO World Heritage Sites in Ilocos Sur allow visitors to travel back in time. Vigan, the capital of Ilocos Sur, is described by UNESCO as "the best-preserved example of a planned Spanish colonial town in Asia," while Santa Maria Church is one of the country's most memorable architectural designs, capturing the European Baroque style and adapting it to the Philippine environment.



Views

Because Ilocos Sur sits on the shore, the Spaniards built multiple watchtowers to protect the province's residents from pirates. The Bantay Bell Tower and the Bateria Watchtower are two of these towers that have lasted the test of time and are still standing today, and the towns of Bantay and San Esteban have opened them to tourists, respectively. Make sure to see the Bantay Bell Tower for an unrivaled view of Vigan, as well as the Bateria Watchtower for a beautiful view of the sea!.



Natural Wonders

After you've had your fill of history, don't forget to visit some of Ilocos Sur's natural wonders. Between its two towering formations, Pinsal Falls presents a picture-perfect image of the best that Ilocos Sur's environment has to offer, while Mapisi Rock allows tourists to thoroughly immerse themselves in nature. They're both examples of the country's natural beauty!.



Heirtage Homes

Extend your immersion in history by reserving a stay in one of Vigan's many rustic-style cottages. Some of the city's centuries-old ancestral homes have been converted into inns, providing you a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to sleep in a piece of history!
